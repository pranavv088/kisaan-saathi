Overview & Production

Overview & Production

Sugarcane is India's primary sugar source, making it a vital cash crop. India is the world's largest consumer and second-largest producer after Brazil. Around 280,000 farmers cultivate sugarcane on 440,000 acres, supporting over 110 million people in the sugar industry. Total cultivation: 3.93 million hectares; Annual production: 170 metric tons; Productivity: 67 tons per hectare.

Climate & Soil

Climate, Soil & Varieties

Sugarcane thrives in warm, humid climates; growth halts above 50°C and slows below 20°C. Optimal rainfall: 750–1,200 mm; excessive rain lowers juice quality. Prefers medium-heavy soils, but lighter soils work with proper irrigation. Popular varieties: Co 419, Co740, Co7219 (Sanjivan), etc. High-quality seed treatment prevents diseases.

Planting Guide

Planting & Irrigation

Planting seasons: Suru (Jan-Feb), Ad Shali (Jul-Aug), Pre-seasonal (Oct-Nov). Fertilization includes organic manure, ammonium sulfate, or oil cakes per seasonal needs. Water needs: 2,000–2,500 mm; critical phases: germination & maturity. Irrigation: Weekly during formative stages; mulching reduces evaporation

Crop Care

Crop Care & Pest Control

Weeding: Done 3–4 weeks after planting; final earthing-up before monsoon rains. Tying canes prevents lodging from strong winds. Major pests: Termites & shoot borers. Diseases: Red rot & whip smut; local agricultural experts offer control measures.

Harvesting

Harvesting Process

Maturity signs: Fewer green leaves on top; refractometer reading of 20 indicates readiness. Small Mill Test (SMT): Cutting 10 canes, crushing them, and lab-testing for purity & CCS %. Harvesting begins: November 1st, lasting 150 days; done per factory agreements.

Mechanization

Mechanization & Yield Expectations

Modern harvesters: Use rotating knives, trim tops, and remove excess foliage with extraction fans. Cut into 12-inch "bullets", then transported via wagons. Yield: Commercial crop: 100 tons/hectare (11–12-month-old plants). Suru crop: 170 tons/hectare. Adali Pre-seasonal crop: 120 tons/hectare.
